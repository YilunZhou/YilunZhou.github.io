ExSum: The Explanation Summary Framework for Deriving
Generalized Model Understandings from Local Explanations

Yilun Zhou1, Marco Tulio Ribeiro2, Julie Shah1
1 MIT CSAIL, 2 Microsoft Research
[Paper]    [GitHub Repo]    [Package Documentation]
NAACL 2022

TLDR: Useful local explanations need to be both correct and easily understandable. While the definition and evaluation of correctness have received much attention, those on understandability is mostly overlooked. We present ExSum, the first framework to formally quantify and evaluate the understandability of local explanations. Experimental results demonstrate that we indeed stand to benefit from such a careful analysis.

Code: We provide an easy-to-use package for you to play with the ExSum experiments described in the paper and use it for your models and datasets. Just pip install exsum and follow the documentation to get started.

Abstract: Interpretability methods are developed to understanding the working mechanisms of black-box models, which is crucial to their responsible deployment. However, fulfilling this goal requires both that the explanations are correct and that people can correctly and easily understand these explanations. While much work has studied the former, people often overlook the latter and obtain quite informal understandings from a handful of local explanations. In this paper, we introduce Explanation Summary (ExSum), a mathematical framework to quantify such understandings and propose metrics for quality assessment. On two domains, ExSum highlights various limitations in the current practice of model understandings, helps with extracting better ones, and reveals hidden model properties. Last, we connect the notion of understandability to other commonly mentioned and desired properties for explanations such as human alignment, robustness, and counterfactual minimality and plausibility.
